What does this mean?

The Overall Bias Score (0–100) represents the aggregated level of bias detected across multiple dimensions in the content, response, or decision.

0 – 25 Low Bias Minimal to no signs of bias.
26 – 50 Moderate Bias Some bias present; review recommended.
51 – 75 High Bias Significant bias detected; action needed.
76 – 100 Severe Bias Strong bias detected; immediate mitigation recommended.

Context: Why Bias Matters

Bias can influence decisions, recommendations, and interactions in ways that may be unfair, inaccurate, or harmful. It can affect individuals and groups, reduce trust, and lead to unethical or inequitable outcomes.

Common impacts include:

  • Unfair treatment of individuals or groups
  • Reduced diversity and inclusion
  • Loss of trust and credibility
  • Legal and reputational risks
  • Poor decision quality

How to Mitigate Bias

1. Awareness

Understand your own biases and how they can influence outcomes.

2. Diversify Data & Perspectives

Use inclusive, representative data and seek multiple viewpoints.

3. Review & Validate

Regularly audit outputs and decisions for bias using metrics and human review.

4. Apply Fairness Techniques

Use debiasing methods, constraints, and fairness-aware models where possible.

5. Iterate & Monitor

Continuously monitor bias scores and improve processes over time.
