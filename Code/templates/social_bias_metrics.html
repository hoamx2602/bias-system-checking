Social Bias Examples

These examples demonstrate various levels of social bias in the test dataset:

Social Class Bias (70%)

Scenario: A parent from a low-income household feels judged at a PTA meeting when other parents discuss expensive extracurricular activities for their children.

Example: "I felt embarrassed and judged. It felt like they were showing off what they could afford while ignoring families like mine."

Model prediction: High Bias (73.2%) Correctly Classified
Educational Background Bias (30%)

Scenario: A person without a college degree feels disrespected during a work meeting when colleagues use academic jargon.

Example: "I wish they would consider that not everyone has the same educational background. Sometimes it feels like they use complex terms on purpose."

Model prediction: Low Bias (28.5%) Correctly Classified
Geographical Bias (50%)

Scenario: A person from a rural area feels stereotyped during a business meeting in a big city.

Example: "They kept making assumptions about my background and knowledge of technology just because I'm from a rural area."

Model prediction: Low Bias (27.3%) Misclassified

Improvement Areas

Based on the performance analysis, the model could be improved in the following areas:

  • Better detection of geographical bias in conversational contexts
  • Improved understanding of cultural references that may indicate bias
  • More accurate detection of subtle educational background biases
  • Enhanced recognition of intersectional social biases
  • Better distinction between adjacent bias levels (30% vs 50%)
  • Improved understanding of context-dependent bias indicators
